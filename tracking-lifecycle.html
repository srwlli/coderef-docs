From idea to shipped

The lifecycle of tracked entities

CodeRef tracks four entity kinds — stubs, workorders, dispatches, and tickets. They form a pipeline from "an idea worth remembering" to "work that shipped," plus a parallel lane for bugs.

Stub — the lightweight idea

A stub (STUB-XXXXXX) is the smallest unit: a captured idea, improvement, or fix target that isn't ready to be full work yet. It has a category (feature, improvement, fix, and friends), a priority, an owner_domain, and a status that starts at stub. Stubs are the backlog — cheap to mint, easy to dedup, and the entry point to the workorder pipeline.

Workorder — the unit of execution

A workorder (WO-...) is a stub promoted into real, plannable work: it has phases, a plan, a communication record, and a close-out. Stubs feed workorders via /create-workorder --from-stub=<STUB-ID>. A workorder moves through plan, execute, review, close — and a workorder isn't done until its branch is merged to main and deleted.

Dispatch — the unit of coordination

A dispatch (DISPATCH-<date>-<DOMAIN>-NNN) is a message of work or information routed from one agent domain to another through the daily session. It is a small state machine:

  • pending — filed in the receiver's inbox, not yet seen.
  • accepted — the receiver picked it up and is working it.
  • completed — done, with a result summary written back onto the dispatch.
  • blocked — the side-exit: it needs a decision or unblock before it can finish.

Agents poll their inbox, accept a dispatch, do the work, and complete it. A dispatch marked blocked is the "andon cord" — it surfaces a decision back to the coordinator rather than guessing. Every transition is written by the canonical runner, never by hand (see Views-vs-DB discipline).

Ticket — the bug lane

A ticket (TKT-XXXXXX) is the structured record for a bug: severity (impact), priority (scheduling), an error_kind, and a repro. Filing a ticket auto-mints a linked category=fix stub, so a bug flows straight into the same workorder pipeline.

Tickets have their own guarded state machine — new, triaged, in_progress, resolved, verified, closed, with side-exits duplicate, wontfix, and cannot_reproduce. The guard that matters most: resolved and verified require evidence — a real artifact (commit hash, file path, pasted output), never a fabricated claim.

The shape of the whole flow

  • An idea becomes a stub; a stub is promoted to a workorder; the workorder produces commits; the work ships when the branch is merged and the workorder closes.
  • A bug becomes a ticket, which auto-mints a fix stub — feeding the same pipeline.
  • At every stage, coordination between domains travels as a dispatch (pending to accepted to completed, with blocked as the escalation lane).

Canonical sources

  • /stub, /create-workorder, /ticket — the minting skills (each uses the canonical DB-insert path).
  • TRACKING/workorders.json, TRACKING/stubs.json, TRACKING/tickets.json — the generated views of each lane.
