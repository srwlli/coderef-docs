What a skill is

A map and a guide

A skill is a map and a guide, not a script. It tells an agent what a workflow is for, when to reach for it, and the exact contract to honor — and it points at depth instead of inlining it. The agent stays the executor; deterministic mechanics live in runners the skill ships.

That division of labor is the heart of the system: prose carries judgment and context, runners carry mechanics, and neither pretends to be the other. A skill that needs determinism ships a runner; a skill that is pure guidance ships prose; both present the same contract surface to the agent.

The taxonomy

The corpus currently holds 168 skills across 17 purpose categories:

ANALYSIS, CONTENT, DOCUMENTATION, WORKFLOW, SESSION, GENERATION, INTEGRATION, KAIZEN, STANDARDS, SURFACES-HTML, TRACKING, UTILITIES, ON-TASK, ENTITY, CORE, PRIMARY-SOURCES, DEBUG.

The category enum is schema-canonical — defined in TRACKING/schemas/skills.schema.json — and directories, manifests, and the registry all derive from it. A category isn't a folder someone liked; it's a schema value the whole system validates against.
